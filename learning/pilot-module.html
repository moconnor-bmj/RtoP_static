ECG skills

Welcome to BMJ Learning’s ECG skills course. This course is for all healthcare professionals who need to conduct and interpret electrocardiograms (ECGs).

Using a mixture of 3D animation, audio narrative, images, detailed text, and clinical cases, this course will take you through the fundamentals of ECGs, including:

  • Basic cardiac anatomy and physiology
  • The practicalities of making an ECG trace
  • A solid system for interpreting ECGs
  • Problems of rate and rhythm, including bradycardia, tachycardia and atrial fibrillation
  • Myocardial infarction
  • Diagnostic testing
  • Cardiac muscle disease and congenital heart disease.

Each condition is illustrated with high resolution ECGs which can be interactively zoomed and measured on your screen. The modules conclude with multiple choice questions that will test your learning and help you to gain further confidence in your skills.

This course was developed in collaboration with a team of specialists based at Sheffield Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust and all content has been externally peer reviewed.

Biography :

Kevin Channer was appointed in 1989 as a general physician and cardiologist to the Royal Hallamshire Hospital, part of Sheffield Teaching Hospitals NHS Trust. He was clinical director of medicine at the Royal Hallamshire Hospital from 1994 to 1998 and deputy clinical director until April 2002.

Biography :

He is an honorary clinical senior lecturer in medicine for the University of Sheffield Medical School, and was appointed Honorary Professor of Cardiovascular Medicine at Sheffield Hallam University in July 2004. Since he retired from the NHS in 2012 he has been appointed to the Council of the Medical Defence Union (MDU) and continues to provide expert advice for HM coroners, and the Court in both criminal and civil cases.
